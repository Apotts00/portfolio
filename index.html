Case Study: Payroll System Modernization

Red Rover Implementation ‚Ä¢ Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools

At CMS, the payroll process used to be completely paper-based. Staff filled out handwritten forms for absences, time corrections, and time sheets, which then had to be manually entered and stored for five years to meet audit requirements. The system was time-consuming, error-prone, and confusing for both staff and administrators.

When the district adopted the Red Rover workforce management system, I took ownership of helping our team transition from paper to a digital process. I learned the platform myself, then led staff through the change so payroll could become more accurate, transparent, and efficient.

My Role & Impact

  • Served as the bridge between staff and leadership during rollout
  • Trained 60+ staff to create absences, upload documentation, and track PTO
  • Helped staff view schedules, missed punches, and time sheets in real time
  • Troubleshot technical and visibility issues during the transition
  • Improved accuracy of missing clock-ins/outs on time sheets
  • Reduced manual corrections and streamlined payroll processing

Outcomes

  • More accurate and consistent payroll data
  • Greater transparency for staff around PTO and time off requests
  • Fewer manual corrections and less time spent chasing paper
  • Smoother communication between employees and administrators

This project is a strong example of how I lead system implementations: I quickly learn new tools, support users through change, and focus on making processes easier and more reliable for everyone involved.
